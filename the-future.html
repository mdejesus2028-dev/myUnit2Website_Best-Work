The Future

Reflection:

Some skills I have learned are definitely learning to not go for the most complex approach for a project, especially when I know my complex idea would be time-consuming during a period where I have no space to devote that much time to (especially during a busy week when I should devote my time to sports & other tests/projects). As a perfectionist aiming to create the best project ever (to appeal to my classmates and my appeal to aesthetics), I had to develop the skill of knowing when to go for the simpler option. Additionally, I’ve learned how to code using App Lab, Game Lab, and HTML/CSS, though it required going through paths of major confusion, hours of screen-staring, and a lot of trial-and-error. Moreover, I’ve learned how to use outside resources, whether that was my friends, youtube for tutorials, or old assignments teaching me on how to do the fundamentals. Lastly, I’ve learned how to present in front of a group of students, as some projects required an in-class presentation. After the first presentation, I became super comfortable talking in front of my class to discuss my processes and overall description of my CS projects.

I also learned that I cannot rely on doing a project in a single day, even if I have an entire day to dedicate to that one project, because doing so will end up in me sleeping past 3am or pulling an all nighter. Some areas of weakness are definitely HTML/CSS coding and planning out my time efficiently (because I can spend a whole 1 hour 15 min class just looking up for the most appealing images to use in my website [I did in fact], and I spent my first all-nighter in CS by doing a project that night), when I could’ve coded a third of my website in that time. And I can’t even afford to dedicate so much time to such an insignificant portion of my project when my plate is constantly full of other responsibilities.

Ultimately, these newfound skills can definitely help me in other subjects: I can use my realization in having to pick a battle for complexity or simplicity for class projects; I can now confidently present in front of a group of people to address a topic; and I’ve learned how to solve my own problems when I’m stuck.

In regards to my future, I do not have any goals related to computer science. I want to work in the medical field in the future, though I am still exploring the various fields within pre-med. On the other hand, though this class was definitely time-consuming and, at times, complicated, I see myself incorporating the obtained CS knowledge from this course into the tasks of whichever workforce I choose to do, as the techno-savvy future seems promising.
